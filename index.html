Defeat The Dragon

by: Pallav Achar

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

A few years ago, I watched a series called Merlin, and it was about the Medieval Era with all of the mythical creatures. I really enjoyed this series, and when I had the choice to make any game I wanted, the castle theme came into my head. I have always wanted to create a game with castles, dragons, and knights. I have made a simple version using these characters, but it satisfies me. I am very happy that I was able to complete what I wanted.

2. What I Learned

My teachers and classmates said that the concept of my game was good, and fighting the main boss was a very cool aspect of the game. I originally had a riddle that you had to solve or else you would lose the game. My teacher said that the riddle could be used better; instead of ending the game if they get it wrong, you can give them a power-up if they get it right. That made my game much better. Also, creating a win screen at the end was something they advised which enhanced my game.

3. Architecture of My Game

The knight is controlled by the arrow keys and can be boosted using the key "Q", and you would throw the sword by pressing the space bar. You would try to defeat the dragon using the sword. The sword followed the knight, and the dragon chased the knight. I used eclipse for this game. I also had different knight, dragon, and sword classes to make this all happen, as well as object manager classes to check their collision. I had a game panel class which drew everything and brought everything together.

4. Technical Implementation

I was able to implement most of my ideas into eclipse, creating the knight, sword, and dragon, and importing graphics for them instead of using basic shapes. I am very proud of the sword throwing, because it is the hardest concept to create, but it came out very well. It moves in a specific direction, hits the dragon, and then come backs. This reduces the dragon's health by 10. The sword was very difficult to create, and it is something I am quite proud of. I first planned and brainstormed to accomplish my goal. I then started creating my game, and adding and taking out things that were too difficult or did not fit my game right. I ended up finishing it how I liked, and then I had my peers evaluate it. They told me to fix a few parts, so I did and finished the final game.

5. Most Challenging Part

The most challenging part of this project was creating the sword. Also, ideas to add on to the game were difficult to get. To overcome the sword problem, I asked my teachers for help and they really explained what each aspect of the sword throwing does. We used booleans, longs, and different states to this. I understand how to create a sword and throw it, which will help me in the future if I made a shooter game. To fix the idea problem, I wrote down all of my ideas, and I chose the ideas that weren't too complicated. I was able to accomplish what I wanted.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

I would definitely make more levels with smaller bosses that lead up to the final boss. I would like to make it more realistic, and in a 1st person perspective. I can also give the final boss more ability such as spitting fire balls. I feel like this game has a lot of potential, and I can definitely make it much better in the future.
